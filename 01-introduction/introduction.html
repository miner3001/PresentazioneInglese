Introduction

Why art, music, and video games matter as vehicles for social transformation.

Creativity as a Force for Change

Throughout human history, art has never been just decoration. It has been a weapon, a mirror, a megaphone. From cave paintings that preserved collective memory to protest songs that toppled regimes, creative expression has always been deeply intertwined with the way societies evolve, resist, and transform.

Music, visual art, and — in recent decades — video games have served as powerful tools for challenging injustice, giving voice to the unheard, and imagining new possibilities for the world.

Art has long served as a visual language of resistance — from murals to street installations.

Banksy — Love is in the Air

Also known as the Flower Thrower, this iconic stencil by the anonymous street artist Banksy first appeared in 2003 on a wall near the Israeli West Bank barrier in Jerusalem. It depicts a masked protester in a throwing pose — but instead of a Molotov cocktail, he hurls a bouquet of flowers.

The piece is a powerful statement about peaceful resistance: replacing violence with beauty, destruction with hope. It has become one of the most recognisable works of political street art in the world, symbolising the idea that art itself can be an act of defiance.

Why Does Art Drive Social Change?

It Makes the Invisible Visible

Art gives form to emotions and injustices that words alone often cannot capture. A single image or melody can convey what a thousand speeches cannot.

It Unites Communities

Shared songs, murals, and stories create a sense of belonging and collective identity — essential fuel for any social movement.

It Builds Empathy

Video games and interactive media place us in someone else's shoes, fostering understanding across cultural and social divides.

It Challenges Power

Satire, protest music, and subversive art have historically been among the first targets of censorship — proof of their disruptive power.

What This Presentation Covers

In the following chapters, we will explore how different art forms — music, visual art, and video games — have shaped some of the most important social movements in history. We will look at:

  • The historical roots of art as protest, from ancient times to the 20th century.
  • How music genres like jazz, folk, punk, and hip-hop became soundtracks of resistance.
  • The role of visual art and street art in activism and political messaging.
  • How video games have emerged as a new artistic medium tackling social themes.
  • The impact of the digital age on creative activism and global reach.
